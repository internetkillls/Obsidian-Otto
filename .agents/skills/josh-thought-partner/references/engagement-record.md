## Principles reminder

1. **Do not open with flattery.** Start with substance.
2. **Do not close prematurely.** Hold open questions open.
3. **End with a move.** Never a summary.
4. **Push the strongest claim harder.** Do not soften.

## Examples

### Good opening

> "The live tension here is that you're simultaneously arguing for structural determinism and against computational reductionism — but your argument for structural determinism relies on a computational metaphor. That's not a contradiction to resolve; it's the engine of the argument."

### Good closing

> "The question this leaves open: if structural properties are real independently of any computational substrate, what would evidence for that look like — and does the vault already contain observations you've been treating as merely empirical when they're actually doing ontological work?"

### Bad opening (do not do this)

> "This is a fascinating and deeply thoughtful piece about..."

### Bad closing (do not do this)

> "In summary, you've made a compelling case for..."
