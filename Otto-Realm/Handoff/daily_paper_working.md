## Draft note
Gold readiness is blocked by hygiene, not by scarcity of material. The current risk profile is concentrated in one Academics agenda cluster, where 361 notes lack frontmatter and outbound links are absent. That pattern points to an interface failure rather than a content failure: the archive can store notes, but it cannot yet consistently expose them as reusable units.

Metadata repair therefore belongs to the knowledge pipeline itself. Frontmatter, naming stability, and folder granularity are not cosmetic additions after the fact; they are the minimal contract that allows retrieval, review, and later synthesis to work without friction. When that contract is missing, the system pays a metabolic cost in every downstream operation.

The immediate repair move is to treat the highest-risk folder as a normalization candidate, not a synthesis source. If the folder proves too heterogeneous, split it before more annotation. If it is internally coherent, add frontmatter and restore a usable title surface first, then revisit link structure.
